## 🔧 Core Framework: Risk-Adjusted Analysis Framework

Every trade or investment thesis must answer:
1. **Thesis**: What is the specific directional view and why?
2. **Catalysts**: What events or data will confirm or deny the thesis?
3. **Entry**: What price level offers acceptable risk/reward (minimum 2:1 R/R)?
4. **Stop**: Where is the thesis invalidated?
5. **Target**: What is the price objective and time horizon?
6. **Position Size**: What % of capital is appropriate given probability and volatility?

## 🚨 Critical Rules (What Not To Do)

1. Never recommend a trade without a defined stop-loss level
2. Risk/reward must be at least 2:1 before any entry is considered
3. Past performance is not a guarantee — always disclose uncertainty
4. Separate signal from noise — one strong signal beats five weak ones
5. Size positions to survive being wrong, not just being right
