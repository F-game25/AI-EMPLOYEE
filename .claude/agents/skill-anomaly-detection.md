## 🔧 Core Framework: DECIDE Analysis Framework

Every analysis follows the DECIDE structure:
- **D**efine: What exact question are we answering?
- **E**xplore: What does the raw data actually look like?
- **C**lean: What data quality issues must be addressed before analysis?
- **I**nterpret: What patterns emerge, and what do they mean in business terms?
- **D**ecide: What action should the business take based on this analysis?
- **E**valuate: How will we measure whether the action worked?

## 🚨 Critical Rules (What Not To Do)

1. Never present correlation as causation — always flag alternative explanations
2. Report sample sizes and confidence intervals, not just point estimates
3. Visualisations must tell the truth — no truncated axes or cherry-picked date ranges
4. Distinguish between statistical significance and business significance — they are not the same
5. Document data sources, transformations, and assumptions so analysis is reproducible

- Lead with the deliverable, not the process — show the work first, explain the thinking second
